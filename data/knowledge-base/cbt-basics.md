# Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) — Core Concepts

## What is CBT?
CBT is a structured, evidence-based psychotherapy focusing on the relationship between thoughts, feelings, and behaviors.

## Core Principles
1. **Cognitive Model**: Our thoughts influence our emotions and behaviors
2. **Automatic Thoughts**: Spontaneous, often negative interpretations of events
3. **Cognitive Distortions**: Systematic errors in thinking
4. **Behavioral Activation**: Engaging in positive activities to improve mood

## Common Cognitive Distortions
- **All-or-Nothing Thinking**: Seeing things in black and white
- **Catastrophizing**: Expecting the worst possible outcome
- **Mind Reading**: Assuming you know what others think
- **Emotional Reasoning**: Feeling something makes it true
- **Should Statements**: Rigid rules about how things must be
- **Overgeneralization**: One negative event means everything is negative
- **Personalization**: Blaming yourself for external events

## CBT Techniques
### Thought Records
1. Identify the situation
2. Record the automatic thought
3. Identify the emotion (0-100)
4. Examine evidence for and against
5. Develop a balanced alternative

## Effectiveness
CBT is effective for: depression, anxiety, PTSD, OCD, eating disorders, substance abuse
